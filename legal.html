9. CONTROLS FOR DO-NOT-TRACK FEATURES

Most web browsers and some mobile operating systems and mobile applications include a Do-Not-Track ("DNT") feature or setting you can activate to signal your privacy preference not to have data about your online browsing activities monitored and collected. At this stage, no uniform technology standard for recognizing and implementing DNT signals has been finalized. As such, we do not currently respond to DNT browser signals or any other mechanism that automatically communicates your choice not to be tracked online. If a standard for online tracking is adopted that we must follow in the future, we will inform you about that practice in a revised version of this Privacy Notice.

California law requires us to let you know how we respond to web browser DNT signals. Because there currently is not an industry or legal standard for recognizing or honoring DNT signals, we do not respond to them at this time.

Safety Guidelines

Last updated: October 2025

California Compliance Notice

RoomThere is a social connection platform only. We facilitate introductions between members but do not participate in, supervise, or manage rental agreements, financial arrangements, or living terms. All agreements are made directly between members.

Before You Connect

  • Always communicate through RoomThere until you establish trust
  • Our messaging system protects your personal contact information
  • Report any suspicious behavior immediately through our platform
  • California Law Compliance: We maintain records as required by California consumer protection laws
  • Look for verification badges on profiles
  • Review profile completeness and authenticity
  • Check member references when available
  • Always have at least one video call before meeting in person using free services like Google Meet or Zoom
  • Verify identity and establish comfort level through video before exchanging personal information
  • Discuss expectations and preferences virtually first

Meeting In Person

  • First meetings should be in public spaces like coffee shops, libraries, or community centers
  • Inform a friend or family member about your meeting plans
  • Share the date, time, location, and profile information
  • Your comfort and safety come first
  • If something feels wrong, politely end the conversation
  • You are never obligated to proceed if you feel uncomfortable
  • When touring a home, consider bringing a companion
  • Ensure someone knows your exact location and expected return time
  • Tour during daylight hours when possible

Platform Safety Features

  • Optional background checks available through accredited partners
  • California Compliance: All background checks comply with California Civil Code Â§1786.22
  • RoomThere never shares your personal contact information
  • Secure data storage and transmission
  • California Compliance: We adhere to CCPA/CPRA requirements for data privacy

Creating Safe Connections

  • Discuss preferences and boundaries upfront
  • Create clear understanding about shared spaces and household norms
  • Define communication preferences
  • RoomThere does not handle financial transactions
  • Members arrange financial terms directly
  • Use traceable payment methods
  • California Law: All rental agreements must comply with California Civil Code
  • Exchange emergency contact information voluntarily
  • Discuss emergency procedures if cohabitating
  • Know local resources and emergency contacts

Red Flags to Watch For

  • Pressures you to move quickly or skip safety steps
  • Refuses to participate in video calls or meet in public
  • Asks for money outside of agreed-upon arrangements
  • Provides inconsistent information or avoids specific questions
  • Seems uncomfortable with verification processes
  • Makes you feel pressured or uncomfortable
